# Care Passport — Output Specification

## Output Design Principle

Outputs should feel appropriate in multiple support settings.

A reader should be able to imagine using the document in:

- residential aged care
- home support
- disability support
- hospital care
- rehabilitation
- respite care

without the document feeling tied to a specific environment.

Outputs should be polished, readable, respectful and suitable for families to print and share.

## Outputs Required for MVP

The MVP must generate three PDFs:

1. Poster A — Who I Am
2. Poster B — Helpful Things to Know When Supporting Me
3. Full Booklet

## Poster A — Who I Am

Purpose: quick personal orientation.

Designed to be read at a glance.

Likely content areas:

- portrait photo
- preferred name
- short personal tagline
- what to call me
- my life in brief
- talk to me about
- things that help me feel good or comfortable
- please know

## Poster B — Helpful Things to Know When Supporting Me

Purpose: practical person-centred support preferences.

This must not be titled or framed as a care plan.

Likely content areas:

- mornings
- evenings and sleep
- communication style
- if I seem upset
- things to ask me first
- comfort items or important routines

## Full Booklet

Purpose: fuller personal identity and support passport.

The booklet should include all non-private answered questions in a coherent structure.

Skipped questions must not create awkward empty headings or gaps.

Private answers must not appear in the booklet or posters.

## Visibility Rules

- Poster visibility: answer may be used in poster outputs.
- Booklet visibility: answer may appear in the booklet only.
- Private visibility: answer is retained in the app as a private family record but excluded from all printed outputs.

## Language Rules

Do not use:

- care plan
- instructions for staff
- required care
- staff must
- care directives

Use:

- helpful things to know
- what helps me feel comfortable
- information that may help people understand me
- preferences and routines
- helpful things to know when supporting me

## PDF Quality Requirement

The output PDFs must be visually polished.

They should not look like raw form exports.

They must be suitable for home printing and for sharing with carers, support workers, healthcare providers, or a residential aged care facility.
