# Clinical Boundaries and Language Rules

## Core Boundary

Life & Care Passport is a person-centred identity and preferences package. It is not a clinical care plan.

The app must avoid any wording that suggests outputs are binding instructions, clinical directives, professional assessments, or facility requirements.

## Acceptable Framing

Use language such as:

- helpful things to know
- what helps me feel comfortable
- information that may help staff understand me
- preferences and routines
- things that matter to me
- ways people can help me feel settled
- information from me and my family

## Avoided Framing

Do not use:

- care plan
- instructions for staff
- required care
- staff must
- directive
- mandated
- clinical assessment
- treatment plan
- behaviour support plan

## Required Disclaimer

All generated PDFs should include a concise disclaimer:

> This document is intended to help people understand the person behind the resident. It shares personal history, preferences and routines. It is not a clinical care plan and does not replace assessment, care planning, facility procedures or professional judgement.

## Tone

The tone should be:

- respectful
- plain-language
- non-adversarial
- warm but not sentimental
- practical
- suitable for families and aged care staff

## Facility Relationship

The product should not imply facility permission is required for a family to print and share the documents. However, it must frame the documents as helpful information offered respectfully, not as demands.
